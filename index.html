Mission , Vission and Broader Context

1) Develop and demonstrate new technologies and processes to fabricate a solvent free halide based perovskite solar cells.

2) To be a global leader in producing halide based perovskite solar modules by utilizing dry toner technology aided by ink jet printing.

3) Aid in supporting long-term goal of decarbonisation by providing safe and sustainable solutions in the realm of renewables.

Our mission at BM Renewables is to provide safe, sustainable, reliable and innovative technological solutions in the ever evolving energy sector (solar power, green hydrogen and fuels), driven primarily by end-user needs, patterns and government policies and regulations.

BM Renewables is an Australian company with global presence championing in fabricating halide based perovskite photovoltaic cells and modules using a solvent free approach. We are striving to enhance the power conversion efficiencies (performance) by innovating in processing steps to realise a fully flexible, modular and reliable solar cells by lowering the levelized cost of energy (LCOE).

Climate change in form of intense heat, drought, extreme rainfall leading to floods, stronger storms and hailstorms have had unprecedented harmful impact on communities in Australia and around the globe. We strive to work across professional and academic disciplines, connecting different skillsets to attain a collaborative technological solution, enabling us in contributing to the zero carbon emissions target.

About Us

Hemant Kumar Mulmudi

FOUNDER DIRECTOR

Hemant is the founder and director of BM Renewables Pvt Ltd based in Canberra. Dr. Mulmudi obtained his PhD in materials science (2014) from the Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, his MS in nanotechnology from the Ulm University, Germany (2009), and his bachelor’s degree in metallurgical and materials engineering (2007) from the Indian Institute of Technology-Roorkee, India. He is an expert in the field of solar energy generation, especially, with the third generation of solar cells such as dye-sensitized solar cells and halide based perovskite solar cells. He worked on cathodoluminescence spectroscopy to unravel the optoelectronic properties of halide based perovskite materials. Dr. Mulmudi also developed cross-sectional nanoindentation technique to evaluate the fracture toughness of single and multi-junction photovoltaic devices at the Australian National University, Canberra. His research highlights include the first demonstration of a low-temperature, solution based processing of flexible solid state perovskite solar cells (Chemical Communications 49 (94), 11089-11091) and also stable lead-free inorganic perovskite solar cell (Advanced Materials 26 (41), 7122-7127). He has published over 35 internationally acclaimed peer-reviewed journals, filed a provisional patent with IP Australia (IP right number: 2018902843) and has co-authored a book titled ‘Perovskite Solar cells: Technology and Practices’. He delivered lectures on photovoltaic technologies as a casual academic at the Australian National University, Canberra.

DINESH TYAGI

Technical Advisor

Dinesh Tyagi graduated from IIT Delhi in 1980 and received his PhD degree from Virginia Tech in 1985. He then joined Eastman Kodak Company as a Research Scientist where he specialized in the field of digital printing and polymer research. He was inducted into Kodak's Distinguished Inventors Gallery in 1994. In 1999, he joined NexPress Solutions, which was later acquired back by Kodak. Dr. Tyagi recently joined Lexmark International where he continues to work in the area of toners and electrophotography as he has done through most of his professional career. Dr. Tyagi has been granted over 300 patents worldwide. In 2011, he was awarded the Chester F. Carlson Award for his innovations and broad contributions to electrophotographic toner technology. He was also awarded the 2014 Robert F. Reed Technology medal by Technical Association of the Graphics Arts for Outstanding Contribution to the Graphics Arts Industry. Dr. Tyagi is known for his contributions to the understanding of lithographic processes and promoting sustainability in business practices associated with printing. He is currently based in Colorado where he directs research activities pertaining to halide based perovskite optoelectronics.

ANDREAS ARNDT

Consultant

Andreas studied physics at the Philipps-Universität Marburg, Germany, and at the University of Alberta, Canada. In his Diploma thesis, he worked on a monolithically grown inorganic laser system on silicon. For his PhD in electrical engineering, he worked on organic semiconductors at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), Germany. In particular, he focused on charge-transfer states in organic solar cells. To enable scientific exchange and ideas, Dr. Arndt visited the Victoria University of Wellington as an Associate. As a scientific associate at KIT he also collaborated with a number of partners form industry, for example he worked with the German chemical company (BASF) providing them advice on formulations for developing perovskite solar cells. Besides his work in academia, Dr. Arndt obtained a MBA fundamentals certificate from Hector School at KIT. The MBA training has enabled him to address, manage and build small start-ups and run businesses in the technology sector. Finally, he has decided to start his career path in the field of intellectual property (IP) and works as a patent attorney's trainee in an IP firm in Hamburg, Germany.
